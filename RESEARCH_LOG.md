## 2026-06-23 — CLOSE v1 — diagnostic confirms task-headroom failure; banked as a clean negative
- Hypothesis: I expect to distinguish "ipsum's implementation is too weak" from "co-change signal is genuinely redundant on this task" by comparing RTPTorrent's own shipped baseline strategies on okhttp — if even the dataset's file-test-failure-matrix strategies fail to beat the historical-failure-rate strategy, the limitation is the task/signal, not ipsum's code.
- Setup: post-hoc analysis (no new ipsum run). For each okhttp baseline schedule in `/Users/bytedance/Downloads/MSR2/square@okhttp/baseline/`, computed the mean fraction of the suite run before the first failing test, over the 749 builds containing a failure. Strategies: `optimal-failure`, `recently-failed` (historical), `matrix-naive` / `matrix-conditional-prob` (file-change), `random`, `untreated`.
- Result: first-failure-at = optimal `0.073`, recently-failed `0.109`, matrix-naive `0.187`, matrix-conditional-prob `0.287`, random `0.513`, untreated `0.519`. File-change signal is real (matrix-naive ≪ random) but historical failure-rate (`0.109`) is far stronger and nearly hits the oracle ceiling (`0.073`), so file-change is redundant with a cheaper feature. This matches ipsum's okhttp funnel (abstraction held-out LL gain max `0.0033`, mean `−0.00006`; 12 admitted, 0 retained, slope gap `0.0`).
- Decision: CLOSE / BANK as a clean negative. Not an engineering failure (signal confirmed real, pipeline fixed and validated), not a thesis refutation (one weak instantiation on a no-headroom task). Root cause: predictive test selection's signal is dominated by historical failure rate, leaving no headroom for accumulated abstractions. Full writeup in `RESULTS.md`.
- Next: none for this instantiation — the signal is not there to tune toward. A future, fair test of the thesis requires a headroom-rich task (cheap features do not saturate the signal) and possibly a richer abstraction substrate than co-change. Testbed, harness, and notes are reusable.
